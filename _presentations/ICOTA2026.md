This presentation was prepared for the International Conference on Optimization: Techniques and Applications (ICOTA 2026), held in Shanghai, China.

**Title:** First-Order Algorithms for Stochastic Multi-objective Optimization Problems

**Abstract:** Stochastic multi-objective optimization (SMOO) problems arise in many applications where several conflicting performance criteria are modelled by expectations, such as prediction accuracy and fairness, or return and risk. In this paper, we study first-order methods for SMOO and develop objective-function-free optimization (OFFO) and scalarization-free algorithms based only on stochastic gradient information. We introduce a Tikhonov-regularized simplex quadratic program to define a single-valued multi-gradient direction and a regularized Pareto-stationarity measure. We establish Lipschitz continuity of the induced multiplier and direction mappings on convex bounded sets. We also show that the regularized stationarity measure converges to the Pareto-stationarity measure as the regularization parameter tends to zero. Based on this construction, we propose an OFFO-type algorithm with a sample-average approximation (SAA) method for SMOO. We establish stationarity-complexity results for the algorithm. Numerical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
